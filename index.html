The idea

Alt-Tab wasn't built for a hundred passes a day.

If your job means moving through the same set of windows over and over — entering data, comparing records, copying between apps — the mental cost isn't the typing. It's finding the next window, again and again. Orvesca removes that. You arrange your windows once into a workflow, then move through them in a fixed, circular order: forward with one key, back with another, or jump straight to any window with a key of your own.

Built around your keyboard

Every move is a keystroke.

What it does

Everything you need to move fast — nothing you don't.

An ordered loop

Arrange your windows into a path and move through them forward or back in a circle. Reorder any time, and duplicate a window when a step repeats.

Jump keys you choose

Give any window a single custom key and jump straight to it. Turn jumping on per window, or off entirely when you need those keys back.

Release & reclaim

One press frees every shortcut so your keys are yours again; press once more to reclaim them — no fighting over hotkeys mid-task.

Notes on any window

Pin a reminder to a specific window — “Calculate the average” — and show or hide it whenever you like.

Saved paths

Save a whole workflow — order, jump keys, jump state, notes and their visibility — and restore it in one click. Free keeps one; Pro keeps five.

Title-change alerts

When a window's title or tab changes, a small notice appears next to that window's card in your workflow to keep you posted — quietly, without getting in your way.

Save & restore

Build the path once. Bring it back tomorrow.

Save an entire workflow to a slot and restore it with a single click — window order, custom jump keys, whether jump is on, your notes, and whether each note is showing. The free plan keeps one path; Pro keeps five.

Notes that stay put

A note that lives on the window it belongs to.

Attach a reminder to any window and it travels with it — visible when you want the nudge, hidden when you don't. Small thing, big relief on a long shift.
